# Methodology

## Project type

Academic business planning project for a Pazarlama Yönetimi (Marketing Management) course at Istanbul University. The deliverable was a comprehensive go-to-market strategy and financial feasibility plan for a startup concept.

## Business concept

ParkLyst: an AI-powered mobile platform enabling real-time parking discovery, reservation, and payment in Istanbul. The plan addresses urban parking congestion through IoT sensor integration, dynamic pricing, and behavioral targeting.

## Analytical framework map

| Framework | Purpose in the project |
|---|---|
| PEST Analysis | Mapped macro-environmental factors (economic, legal, social, technological) |
| Micro-environment analysis | Assessed suppliers, competitors, intermediaries, and customers |
| Porter's Five Forces | Evaluated sector competitive dynamics and entry barriers |
| SWOT Matrix | Identified internal strengths/weaknesses and external opportunities/threats |
| STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning) | Defined target market and brand positioning strategy |
| 7P Marketing Mix | Structured the full marketing program (Product, Price, Place, Promotion, People, Process, Physical Evidence) |
| Break-even Analysis | Calculated the minimum vehicle throughput required to cover costs |
| Gantt Chart | Planned development and launch timeline from feasibility to commercial rollout |

## Segmentation approach

A differentiated marketing strategy was adopted over undifferentiated (mass) marketing:

- **Individual segment:** Urban drivers in Istanbul seeking time savings, convenience, and mobile-first payment
- **Corporate segment:** Businesses, fleet managers, and private parking operators seeking occupancy optimization and revenue analytics
- **Indirect audience:** Municipalities and smart-city planners as partnership and B2G channel

Segmentation criteria spanned geographic (high-congestion zones in Istanbul), demographic (mid-to-upper income, age 25–45), behavioral (tech adoption, visit frequency), and psychographic (eco-conscious, time-sensitive) dimensions.

## Positioning strategy

Comparative positioning against existing Turkish parking apps, which primarily offer static space listings. ParkLyst's differentiating features:

- AI-based routing personalized to traffic conditions and user history
- Dynamic pricing adapting to real-time demand
- Eco-routing algorithm to reduce fuel use and emissions
- Integrated reservation, payment, and loyalty in one flow

## Pricing strategy

Cost-plus approach with market-level entry pricing. The plan includes:

- **Phase 1:** Market-rate pricing to build user habit and acquisition
- **Phase 2:** Premium pricing as brand recognition grows, supplemented by value-added services
- **Dynamic pricing layer:** Demand-responsive pricing to maximize occupancy and yield simultaneously

## Promotional mix

- **Digital advertising:** Google Ads, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, in-app ads
- **Out-of-home:** City billboards and smart bus stop panels
- **Personal selling:** B2B field sales team for corporate partnerships
- **Sales promotion:** First-use free parking, loyalty program, referral system, corporate bundle campaigns
- **Public relations:** Media outreach, sustainability messaging, smart-city positioning
- **Digital marketing:** SEO, targeted social media campaigns, content on Instagram, X, LinkedIn
