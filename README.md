# ParkLyst — Smart Parking Platform Business Plan

Marketing Management course project developing a comprehensive go-to-market strategy and financial feasibility model for ParkLyst, an AI-powered smart parking platform designed to address Istanbul's urban parking problem.

> **Portfolio note:** This was completed as an academic team project at Istanbul University. This repository presents a portfolio edition and does not claim sole authorship of the original work.

## Project snapshot

* **Course:** Marketing Management — Istanbul University
* **Deliverable:** End-to-end business and marketing plan (47 slides)
* **Industry:** Urban mobility / PropTech / Smart city
* **Target market:** Istanbul — individual drivers, corporate fleet users and institutional partners
* **Analysis tools:** SWOT, Porter's Five Forces, PEST, STP, 7P Marketing Mix, financial feasibility analysis and Gantt planning

## Business concept

ParkLyst is a digital platform designed to help drivers find, reserve and pay for nearby parking spaces in real time.

The concept combines:

* IoT-based parking availability data
* AI-supported routing
* Mobile reservation and payment
* Dynamic pricing
* Loyalty and referral systems
* Corporate and institutional partnerships

The platform aims to reduce parking search time, improve parking-space utilization and create a more convenient urban mobility experience.

## Strategic framework

* **Segmentation:** Geographic, demographic, behavioral and psychographic segmentation
* **Targeting:** Differentiated marketing strategy for individual and corporate users
* **Positioning:** ParkLyst is positioned as an integrated urban mobility solution rather than only a parking-space finder
* **Competitive strategy:** Differentiation through technology, convenience, integrated payment and data-driven services

## Marketing mix

The project evaluates ParkLyst through the 7P Marketing Mix:

1. Product
2. Price
3. Place
4. Promotion
5. People
6. Process
7. Physical Evidence

The marketing strategy combines digital advertising, SEO, social media, referral programs, loyalty systems, B2B sales and institutional partnerships.

## Financial model

The financial feasibility model was developed using academic assumptions and projected operating figures.

| Metric                                             | Value         |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ------------- |
| Total projected investment                         | 39,000,000 TL |
| Modeled monthly operating cost                     | 340,000 TL    |
| Hourly parking fee                                 | 40 TL         |
| Daily capacity assumption                          | 500 vehicles  |
| Projected monthly revenue at full modeled capacity | 1,200,000 TL  |
| Projected annual revenue at full modeled capacity  | 14,400,000 TL |

These figures represent scenario-based academic projections rather than audited or live operational results.

## Main findings

* ParkLyst was positioned as an integrated urban mobility solution rather than only a parking-space finder.
* A differentiated marketing strategy was selected to address individual drivers, corporate users and institutional partners with distinct value propositions.
* Technology-based differentiation was built around IoT-enabled parking availability, AI-supported routing, mobile reservation and integrated payment.
* Corporate and institutional partnerships were identified as important channels for improving scale and revenue stability.
* Financial assumptions should be validated through pilot testing before large-scale commercial expansion.

## Business recommendations

1. Launch first in high-density areas of Istanbul where parking demand is strongest.
2. Develop corporate and institutional partnerships early to create more stable revenue opportunities.
3. Differentiate the platform through integrated reservation, payment, routing and loyalty features.
4. Use data analytics to improve occupancy, pricing and customer experience.
5. Validate financial assumptions through pilot testing before large-scale expansion.
